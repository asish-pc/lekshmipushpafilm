About Me

I am Lakshmi Pushpa,

a film director driven by a profound passion for storytelling and creative expression. My journey into the world of cinema began with a childhood dream to create meaningful and impactful films. After completing my postgraduate studies, I decided to dedicate myself entirely to cinema. Financial limitations prevented me from attending formal film institutes, but my determination and resilience propelled me forward. I embraced self-learning and sought the guidance of mentors who became my guiding lights, helping me navigate the path to realizing my dreams. Today, I take immense pride in having directed two short films, Ama and Kombal/The Girl, as well as a feature-length film, Jami/Clan Women. These works reflect my journey, my commitment to impactful storytelling, and my vision as a filmmaker. Through my films, I aim to explore compelling narratives that resonate with audiences and leave a lasting impression. Each step in this journey has brought me closer to fulfilling my dream of creating cinema that inspires and connects with people on a deeper level.
